App permissions
  • Accessibility Service: Detects the package name of the app currently in the foreground in order to display the reminder screen when a monitored app is opened. In supported browsers, the web address currently open is additionally read — exclusively in order to compare it against the block lists you have set up yourself (your own blocked domains or the optional adult content filter). Web addresses are processed only at the moment of that comparison and discarded immediately; they are not stored, not logged and not transmitted. No browsing history, keystrokes, passwords or form contents are recorded.
  • Overlay permission: Display of challenge overlays on top of other apps.
  • Usage statistics: Determining the usage duration of monitored apps in order to evaluate your time-based challenges.
The information processed by the Accessibility Service (app package name, web addresses) remains exclusively on your device. Only the summaries required for your challenges (e.g. usage duration and number of deliberate opens) are transmitted to your account — never web addresses you have visited or any browsing history.
